Two true positives were identified. `zero_is_empty_external_body_trusted` flags a real soundness gap where the foundational property that zero decodes to an empty page entry is axiomatically trusted via external_body rather than verified with bit_vector proofs. `page_entry2usize_no_roundtrip_guarantee` highlights that the encode function's contract lacks an explicit full structural roundtrip guarantee (`spec_usize2page_entry(ret) =~= pe`), though the individual field ensures may be sufficient in practice. The other two candidates are false positives: forcing non-present entries to be empty is intentional design, and the entry identity preservation is directly guaranteed by the set() postcondition.
